    \subsection{Ethical restocking}
    As depicted in this model candidate about ethical restocking, as shown in figure 3, is showing what tasks, resources and goals are influenced by the process. As is visible, there are a lot of agents (e.g., sourcing manager, third-party auditor, etc.) that are needed for a supply chain.
As IKEA is all for ethics, and “Caring for people and planet”  there is a candidate model about ethical restocking. It is known that IKEA is al about and “Caring for people and planet”  (IKEA, 2026). It shows what agents are connected to IKEA regarding their inventory and restocking. The agents are all parts of a bigger mechanism, thus using ethical restocking is a really logical improvement goal.
The sourcing manager (or alternatively procurement manager) is needed for sourcing, negotiating and purchasing goods or services from suppliers. They check if the goods are of a high enough quality. For this they need to select the correct suppliers and create contracts with them. The “correct” suppliers are chosen based on audits from third parties. They investigate if the supplier satisfies the supplier expectations of IKEA. All this data is combined to select approved suppliers.
On the other side of the model, the supplier and sustainability officer are shown. After a supplier is approved, IKEA will buy their goods, and they will be checked by the sustainability officer. They monitor supplier compliance through their supplier data platform. This platform gives transparency about their suppliers and if they (still) meet their contractual obligations/sustainability goals.
Together the sourcing manager and sustainability officer participate in IKEA. IKEA ensures ethical restocking through the data from the sourcing manager and sustainability officer. And because the transparency of the supplier platform gives stakeholders more trust in the organisation, the brand reputation is also maintained. This all combined makes IKEA able to ensure ethical restocking and “Care for people and planet” (IKEA, 2026) even better.


    \section{conclusion}
    IKEA’s sustainability ambitions are enormous; they depend on multiple tasks and goals which together create the direction IKEA wants to move in.
    IKEA has company goals to improve employee health and working environments and also has ambitions to improve the planet for the people that live on it.
    As stated before, IKEA wants to be CO2 neutral by 2030 and wants to accomplish that by continuous monitoring of suppliers, anonymous reporting channels and safety audits.
    CO₂-neutral production and stricter sourcing criteria strengthen IKEA’s reputation and competitive differentiation, yet they may increase costs and put pressure on affordability.
    Ethical restocking further demonstrates the complexity of governing a global supply chain, where sourcing managers, sustainability officers and third-party auditors must collaborate through audits, contracts and data platforms to ensure compliance and maintain stakeholder trust.
